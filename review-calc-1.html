  • Evaluate limits using L'Hôpital's rule (methods to fit expressions into \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\)).
Indeterminate Form How to Rewrite Example Setup Result after Rewriting
\(\frac{0}{0}\) Already in quotient form \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}\) Apply L'Hôpital directly
\(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\) Already in quotient form \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x}{e^x}\) Apply L'Hôpital directly
\(0 \cdot \infty\) Rewrite as \(\frac{\ln x}{1/x}\) or similar \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} x \ln x\) Becomes \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\)
\(\infty - \infty\) Combine into single fraction (e.g. conjugate) \(\lim_{x \to \infty} (\sqrt{x^2+1} - x)\) Becomes \(\frac{0}{0}\)
\(0^0\) Take logs: \(g(x)\ln f(x)\) \(\lim_{x \to 0^+} x^x\) Reduces to \(0 \cdot \infty\)
\(\infty^0\) Take logs: \(g(x)\ln f(x)\) \(\lim_{x \to \infty} (1+1/x)^x\) Reduces to \(\frac{0}{0}\)
\(1^\infty\) Take logs: \(g(x)\ln f(x)\) \(\lim_{x \to \infty} (1+1/x)^x\) Reduces to \(\frac{0}{0}\)
